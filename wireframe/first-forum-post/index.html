First Forum Post
Screen builder note
Three-step flow. Step 1: text prompts. Step 2: photo upload — treated as the expected next step, not an optional extra. Skip is possible but visually buried (small muted link, no button). Step 3: preview card before final post. The photo step comes after writing — once the learner has already invested effort, the bar to upload feels lower. The wall is gated until they post.

Design intention — why the photo step is designed this way
Comes after the writing. Once the learner has invested effort in 6 prompts, the marginal cost of uploading a photo feels lower. They are already in the flow.
Skipping is possible but buried. "Continue without a photo" is a small muted text link — not a button. It is not hidden; it is just not the obvious path. Learners who look for it will find it.
The header does the persuasion. "Students who add a photo get more responses" — a social nudge, not a rule. No guilt, no pressure. Just a reason that is true.
